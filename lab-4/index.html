SCENE III. The same. A room in SHYLOCK'S house.

Enter JESSICA and LAUNCELOT

JESSICA

I am sorry thou wilt leave my father so:
Our house is hell, and thou, a merry devil,
Didst rob it of some taste of tediousness.
But fare thee well, there is a ducat for thee:
And, Launcelot, soon at supper shalt thou see
Lorenzo, who is thy new master's guest:
Give him this letter; do it secretly;
And so farewell: I would not have my father
See me in talk with thee.

LAUNCELOT

Adieu! tears exhibit my tongue. Most beautiful
pagan, most sweet Jew! if a Christian did not play
the knave and get thee, I am much deceived. But,
adieu: these foolish drops do something drown my
manly spirit: adieu.

JESSICA

Farewell, good Launcelot.
Exit Launcelot
Alack, what heinous sin is it in me
To be ashamed to be my father's child!
But though I am a daughter to his blood,
I am not to his manners. O Lorenzo,
If thou keep promise, I shall end this strife,
Become a Christian and thy loving wife.
Exit

Addendum

The structure of this HTML document was chosen to best represent the theatrical script format.
A heading (h3) is used for the scene title to ensure clarity.
Speaker names are bolded b for emphasis, and dialogue is enclosed in blockquotes blockquote to differentiate it from other text.
Stage directions are italicized i to maintain the script’s format.
Line breaks br are used within dialogue for proper readability.
Finally, a footer is included to meet the assignment's requirement of tracking last modifications.

1. Font Choices:

I chose the font Arial, a clean and highly readable sans-serif font. Arial is a widely used web-safe font that ensures consistent appearance across all browsers and devices. It also provides a modern, clean feel for the text-heavy content, which improves overall legibility.

I opted for a sans-serif font because it is typically easier to read on screens compared to serif fonts, which are traditionally used for printed materials. This is particularly important for web pages that contain a lot of text.

2. Sizes of Headings and Main Text Size:

Heading Sizes:
The size of h1 is set to 3em, making it the most prominent element on the page. This size ensures that the title stands out and immediately grabs the user's attention when they first arrive on the page.

h2 is set to 2em, making it slightly smaller than the title but still large enough to differentiate the subsections of the content. The slight reduction in size gives a clear visual hierarchy between the main title and the subsections.

Main Text:
The body text is set to 1.1em to maintain readability while providing a slight emphasis over the default text size. This choice allows for comfortable reading without straining the eyes, especially for users who might have visual impairments or those reading on small screens.

The line height for the body text is set to 1.6, which enhances readability by providing enough space between lines of text. This spacing prevents the text from looking cramped and helps the reader to follow along more easily.

3. Color Choices:

Background Color: I chose a soft grey color, #f4f4f4, for the page background. This light grey is less harsh than a pure white background and provides a more comfortable reading experience, particularly over long periods. It also adds a subtle sense of calmness to the design.

Text Colors:
h1 and h2 headings use a darker grey, #333, to provide a subtle contrast against the light grey background without being as stark as pure black. The dark grey gives the headings prominence while maintaining a modern aesthetic.

The body text is styled with a medium grey color, #555, which contrasts well with the background, ensuring the content remains easy to read without causing visual fatigue.

Footer Text: The footer text color is set to a lighter grey, #888, which allows the footer to blend into the background without drawing too much attention, as it is typically less important content compared to the main text.

4. Margin and Padding Choices:

General Padding: I applied 20px padding to the body element to ensure that the content doesn't touch the edges of the browser window, providing a more visually appealing and spacious layout. This also adds breathing room around the content, making the page feel less cramped.

Header Padding: The header has 20px padding all around to ensure that the title has adequate space, which makes it stand out and feel centered on the page. This also prevents the title from touching the edges of the header section, giving it more prominence.

Section Padding: The section has 20px padding as well, which creates consistent spacing between the content and the section's border. This ensures that the content inside each section doesn't feel too crowded, improving the reading experience.

Margins:
I applied margin-bottom: 30px to the header to create space between the header and the main content section. This ensures the header doesn't feel like it's cramped against the content.

The h2 elements are given margin-top: 20px to provide space between the subsections and the preceding content, making the page feel less dense and more structured.

Paragraphs have a margin-bottom of 20px to give enough space between blocks of text and prevent the content from appearing too dense.

5. Other Visual Enhancements:

Section Styling: The content sections are given a white background, rounded corners, and a subtle shadow to make the text area visually distinct from the background. This gives the content a “card-like” appearance, which creates a clean, modern feel and helps focus attention on the text.

Footer Styling: The footer has a smaller font size (0.9em) and a muted grey color (#888), ensuring that it doesn't compete with the main content. This makes the footer less distracting while still being accessible for users who might want to read it.

6. HTML Abuse:

In my Lab 2, I used the <h2> tag for the main essay title because it visually looked better, but semantically it was incorrect. I have now changed this to an <h1> tag in the HTML, which correctly indicates the primary title of the page.

Additionally, I used <div> elements unnecessarily for structural purposes, but now I’ve replaced those with more appropriate semantic elements like <header>, <section>, and <footer>. This improves both the accessibility and structure of the page.
